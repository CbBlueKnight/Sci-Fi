Future Careers

StemWard

Education in STEM extends beyond the classroom. It provides a skill set that controls our thoughts and actions. STEM education, which combines science, technology, engineering, and math, aids in resolving the problems that the world is currently facing. Let's examine what STEM means globally and how it affects the growth of our society.

STEM education equips individuals with skills that increase their employability and prepare them to meet the current demand for labor. It includes all of one's knowledge and abilities. A well-rounded education benefits greatly from each STEM component. Students gain a comprehensive understanding of the world around them through science. It aids in their development as researchers and critical thinkers. Young people can work in an environment that is full of high-tech innovations thanks to technology. Students can learn how to solve problems and put their knowledge to use in new projects with engineering. When designing solutions, people are able to analyze data, spot errors, and deliberate choices thanks to mathematics. STEM education integrates these fields into a single system. As a result, professionals are prepared to transform society through innovative and long-term solutions.

One of the four tracks in the academic track of the senior high school curriculum in the Philippines is called the STEM Strand, which stands for science, technology, engineering, and mathematics. A STEM education curriculum gets pupils ready for STEM-related college coursework. STEM may be the best strand for you in SHS if you have a preference or talent for studying math, science, or engineering.

STEM is an engaging and difficult area of study. The good news is that it can aid in your career preparation for occupations with high paying potential. STEM positions are in high demand today, providing graduates with a wide range of professional options. Following completion of the STEM strand, the following STEM-related occupations may interest you.

Some excellent STEM occupations, like those of a welder or a manufacturing technician, just require an industry certification or a two-year degree. However, the vast majority of STEM occupations call for post-secondary education.

The STEM fields are fascinating and difficult.

They can aid in preparing you for jobs in relatively plentiful and well-paying industries. For students with post-secondary education, STEM jobs are growing faster than non-STEM jobs. Additionally, the problem-solving and critical thinking abilities acquired when studying STEM courses are transferable to practically all occupations and are really in demand for the majority of them.

AGRICULTURE
The purpose of a degree in agriculture is to provide students with technical knowledge of crop raising, pest management, agricultural chemicals, fertilizers, and other tasks associated with crop production and quality control. Aside from that, you will also acquire useful skills in agribusiness, farm management, and raising livestock. Agriculture might be a good choice for you if you consider yourself to be a certified platito or plantita.

Agriculture careers:
Agriculture Economist
Agriculture Engineer
Animal Scientist
Conservationist
Farm Manager
Soil and Crop Scientist

ENGINEERING
Engineering is the process of making and designing any kind of infrastructure. Yes, we refer to everything, including houses, bridges, cellphones, software, machines, and processes—there is an engineering field just for those things! After graduating from one of these engineering courses and passing the board exam, you can pursue a variety of careers as a licensed engineer. Engineers figure out how things work and find practical uses for scientific discoveries. This is an important role for engineers in the world's adoption of technological innovations.

Engineering careers:
Civil engineer

HEALTH CARE
One of the industries that is expanding at the fastest rate not only in the nation but also globally is healthcare. Due to their high demand, health courses have excellent graduate employability rates. There will always be a need for healthcare as long as people live. The fact that choosing a career in healthcare also has a significant impact on the lives of other people makes it a rewarding and highly valued occupation. You might even land a job with a high salary potential if you follow this path. Some in-demand medical careers you might want to think about are as follows:

INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY
Courses in information technology (IT) train students to be experts in computer application installation, operation, development, maintenance, and administration. Its goal is to give students the skills and knowledge they need to use IT concepts and techniques to solve problems. There are a number of positions that deal with the various aspects of information technology, ranging from engineering to tech support. It is likely that students will succeed in these IT careers if they have strong technical skills in addition to skills in data collection and interpretation.

MATHEMATICS AND SCIENCE
The STEM curriculum is centered on science and math. A valuable asset in the government, business, and technology sectors is an impressive aptitude with numbers. Math and science are fundamentally concerned with problem-solving. To complete their work, many professionals rely on the application of mathematical concepts and scientific methods.

Numerous job openings in the fields of mathematics and science can be found as a result of the growing prominence of big data in business, healthcare, and a number of other sectors. You can work in the following professions in this field:

Pursuing a career in the STEM fields is undoubtedly worthwhile with all of these prospective employment options. The STEM area can be quite difficult, but it's also really thrilling and rewarding. Advantages of the STEM Strand

The STEM strand gives senior high school students exposure to the intertwining disciplines (Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics) to see how they work in real life. The STEM strand:

- Builds resilience in a safe environment by allowing students to experiment and to naturally experience failures as part of the learning process
- Encourages teamwork so students can find solutions to problems, record data, write reports, give presentations, and more
- Teaches kids about the power and importance of technology, especially in today’s digital age
Fosters a habit of adaption when things don’t go as planned.
